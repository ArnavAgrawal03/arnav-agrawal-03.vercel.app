# Reflecting on Morphik's mission

I was speaking with a friend recently, and I told him *with pride* that Morphik just saved a customer half a million dollars. He congratulated me, "You know you just caused a drop in global GDP, right?". Everytime we've signed a contract since then, I've asked myself: is this going to drop the world’s GDP? More often than not, the response has been yes. 

This is concerning to me. The value of a company is defined by the product of i) the value it creates in the world and ii) the portion of that value it is able to capture. \[1\] \[2\] The word *world* here is contentious. If we take it literally, then the first part of the product is negative - meaning the value of my company is negative. If we constrain it to mean the world of the startup (i.e. the team, the customers, and the investors) then the first part is positive. This seems like an obvious distinction, but I still feel nauseous whenever I hear a customer tells me they cut their AP team because of Morphik.

Perhaps the feeling will go away with enough volume. I don't believe, however, that volume is enough to kill the daemon. One way to justify job loss is to use the prisoner's dilemna argument: if we don't do this, someone else will; I'd rather it be us than them. This seems to be the way most other founders who are automating some kind of work seem to think about it. I'm a little uncomfortable with this idea.
