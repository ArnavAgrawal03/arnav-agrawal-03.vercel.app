# Reflecting on Morphik's mission

*Morphik replaced it's 20th job last week. It made me want to clarify my thoughts on our mission and give this writing thing a shot.*

I was speaking with a friend recently, and I told him *with pride* that Morphik just saved a customer a million dollars. *"They fired their entire AP team, bro!"* I told him. He congratulated me, "You know you just caused a drop in global GDP, right?". Everytime we've signed a contract since then, I've asked myself: is this going to drop the world’s GDP? More often than not, the response has been yes. 

This is concerning to me. The value of a company is defined by the product of i) the value it creates in the world and ii) the portion of that value it is able to capture. \[1\] \[2\] The word *world* here is contentious. If we take it literally, then the first part of the product is negative - meaning the value of the company is negative. I don't think that we're creating negative value in the world, though. At the very least we're building interesting, new technology. Then, it must mean that we should constrain the word *world* to mean the world of the startup (i.e. the customers, the team, and the investors). This seems like an obvious distinction, but I've started to feel nauseous whenever I hear a customer tell me they cut their AP team or their admin staff because of Morphik.

Perhaps the feeling will go away with enough volume. I don't believe, however, that desensitization is the answer. I'm not looking to care less about the people we impact. If I allow myself to get desensitized to the percieved negative impact of Morphik, then I could also lose the pulse on how Morphik helps customers - meaning that over time my product decisions would worsen in quality. This is the opposite of what I want. So, the only way to move forward is to confront the nausea.
